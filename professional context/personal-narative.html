Personal Life Reflections

Working Toward a Degree

The day I received my college acceptance letter felt surreal. After years of dreaming of the day I could finally call myself a student again, the moment had arrived. But it wasn’t as simple as starting classes. It took a solid plan to get me back to school. In my early thirties, working full-time and managing a small family, the decision to go back to school was nothing short of a challenge.

I had spent days thinking about continuing my education, but I kept neglecting the idea. At first, I wasn’t even sure it was possible. It wasn’t until I heard a talk by Mary N. Cook titled "Seek knowledge: you have work to do" that I understood how essential it was for my career. The phrase stuck with me.

I enrolled in an online program that allowed flexibility. During Block 1, I had many sleepless nights trying to keep up with coursework while working 8 am to 5 pm, Monday to Saturday. At times, I wanted to give up, but thinking of my goal to support my family gave me strength. I leaned on prayer and sought help from classmates.

Completing Block 1 brought not just relief but also a deeper understanding of my own resilience. This journey taught me that perseverance through difficult times is key to achieving our future goals.

A Childhood Memory

I was twelve the first time I traveled alone from one city to another. Having grown up in a modest neighborhood, I had only heard stories of the beauty of the city. During winter, while my family traveled to our home province, I stayed behind, eager to experience the city’s festivities, parades, and fireworks.

The most memorable moments happened late at night — watching movies, exchanging gifts, staying up to challenge each other to see who could sleep the latest. One Christmas, I stayed up an entire night and the following day without rest. I eventually collapsed at my godmother’s house, exhausted. But after a short recovery, I returned to the party, ready to enjoy it again.

That memory stays with me — the magic of the city, the joy of exploration, and the warmth of community. It taught me the importance of curiosity and embracing new experiences, even in childhood.

Meeting My Spouse

The day I met my partner was quite ordinary. I was buying something from her shop, and curiosity led me to ask if she made the items herself. After that brief interaction, we didn’t speak for months.

One day in October, my brother called me outside to tell me someone was asking for me — it was her. We talked, and I invited her to an evening event. That evening changed my life. We started with light jokes, then discussed school, family, religion, and work. When we shook hands goodnight, I felt something different.

Over time, what started as casual conversation became a deep friendship, then love. There were no grand romantic gestures — just small moments of trust, patience, and laughter. Our relationship grew slowly but steadily.

Now, after years together, building a family and a future, I see how our bond was rooted in the little things. Love isn’t always about sparks; it’s about building something real — together.

“True love is about building a foundation of trust and growing together.”
